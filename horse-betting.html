The Beginner’s Guide to Betting on Horse Racing

If you are a beginner to betting on horse racing, it can easily feel overly complicated or complex. Before you start placing bets, you have to first understand that not all horses are the same. Therefore, they are assigned to compete in different races.

Types of Races

Horses can be assigned to specific races based on their past achievements, breed, and more.
Here are some of the different races you may encounter:

Maiden Races

This is a race where the only horses eligible are those that have never won a race. Once the horse wins the race, he or she no longer qualifies for maiden races.

Claiming Races

The most common type of race, this type of race has the lowest prizes. Therefore, the best horses are rarely seen in claiming races.

Stakes Races

This race often has the biggest and highest prizes. However, only thoroughbred horses that meet the criteria are allowed to join.

Handicap Races

In this race, horses are allocated different weights by the handicapper. A strong horse carries more weight during the race.

Allowance Races

Horses have to meet a few set conditions. They also carry weights based on the number of races or money they won.

Start Small
Start with small stakes and build up your experience as you go. If you find that you are winning, you can gradually increase your stakes. If you are mostly losing, reduce your bets and minimize your losses till you learn the ropes.
02.
Be Selective
There are hundreds of horse races around the world every day. You don’t have to bet on all of them. Look at the statistics and information you have and place wagers only on the races you feel confident about.
03.
Watch The Race
Always watch races, especially the race you bet on. While it is interesting, it also provides a learning opportunity for you as it gives you a proper perspective. How each horse wins or loses will tell you more than the chart showing the results.
04.
Trust Your Gut
While reality has shown us that most winning horses come from the elite trainers at the top end of the betting pool, there are always outliers. If you have a strong intuition or evidence that suggests an underdog might win, go for it.
05.
Avoid Exotics
While exotic bets may be tempting due to the high payout rate, you should always avoid it. Exotic bets have high payouts as the chances of winning are extremely slim. To make a profit, the best way is to build your winnings gradually and patiently.
